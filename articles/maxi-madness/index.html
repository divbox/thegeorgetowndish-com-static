Maxi Madness!

Yes oh yes, it's all about maximizing things this summer with maxi-skirts and -dresses! They work in so many different areas of summertime life - at the beach as a cover-ups or out for brunch on a Sunday afternoon or to a friend's night time rooftop party. Plus, you can dress them up or down and wear them with flats or heels. They're flirty, feminine, flowy and down right fabulous!

They truly can go from poolside to the dinner table, it's merely all about accessorizing it up or down. For a dinner out, pair it with a bit more fancy pieces like a chunky necklace or oversized chandelier earrings and a row of bangles on your wrists and a pair of heels. It's totally up to you and depends on your style. You can wear it simple with diamond stud earrings and jeweled flats or flip flops and a pashmina in hand.

Just remember you want your maxi to fall to the floor! So make sure you don't shorten the length of the dress with a pair of high heels. You want to avoid having your heels show and instead the dress should fall just below your ankles.

I love the Shapeshifter Maxi dress (to the left) from Anthropologie because it incorporates one the season's hottest colors - coral -with cream and has a fun and flirty pattern and flattering neckline. That's the key with maxi dresses, you want to make sure they flatter your figure and not make you resemble a potato sack! Fortunately, many designers caught on to us not wanting to appear shapeless and many of the maxi dresses out this season are extremely flattering.

On a recent visit to Miami I had the pleasure of meeting a local Miami-based swimwear and dress designer, Nikki Poulos! You can see me with Poulos on my left and I'm wearing one of my favorite dresses from her 2010 collection (which I get thousands of compliments on when I wear it). So for all of you online shoppers, make sure pop on to her homepage as I promise you you'll find some amazing maxi dresses at reasonable rates!

I'm also a huge fan of Boston Proper and Victoria's Secret for fabulous maxi dresses.

Now, the maxi skirt. If you're going to try out the maxi skirt, it's important to remember three things: you don't want to clean the streets with your skirt; you want to wear something fitted on the top; and show some skin.

For the first, you want to make sure you hem your maxi skirt so that it's not dragging on the streets as you walk and then you end up with the bottom of your skirt ruined. Take yours in to get it hemmed if need be, you won't regret it.

You also want to remember that since you are wearing something loose on the bottom, that you should wear something fitted on the top. So try a fitted tank top or t-shirt. Finally, show some skin up top since you're covered on the bottom - a deep scoop neck tank top or an off-the-shoulder top. This will flatter your figure by creating a narrower top and wider bottom.

You can totally wear a flowy top with your maxi skirt, you'll just want to add a belt to cinch your waist and draw attention to the tiniest area of your waist (this area differs for everyone). South Moon Under had a huge selection of maxis the last time I was in and the white maxi above is by Ella Moss and available online at Revolve Clothing.

Oh yes, and the maxi craze won't end this summer. Fall in to Autumn with this Peasant Maxi Dress by Rachel Zoe available at Neiman Marcus. It's truly a must have!

Hopefully this will help you find some fabulous maxis to enjoy this season and beyond!
