First Home of JFK and Jacqueline Kennedy

Washington Fine Properties represents the seller of this iconic Georgetown home.

A rare opportunity to own a piece of American history has arrived with the listing of 3321 Dent Pl NW, an exquisitely transformed Georgetown townhouse that seamlessly blends its storied past with modern luxury. The iconic property is listed for $4,595,000 by Matt Ackland and Nancy Taylor Bubes of The NTB Group at Washington Fine Properties.

Originally built in 1948 as one of three colonial revival townhomes in the heart of Georgetown’s West Village, this residence holds a significant place in history as the first home of newlyweds Senator John F. Kennedy and Jacqueline Bouvier Kennedy in December 1953.

During their brief six-month stay, the Kennedys’ presence at Dent Place became a small but significant moment in history. The couple, new to married life, found themselves settling into the rental while Jackie sought a home of their own. Though the couple’s stay was short, their impact on the house was memorable—Jackie made several design changes to the property, such as repainting furniture and hanging John’s paintings on the walls.

Seventy years after Jackie’s initial design projects, the property was completely renovated by renowned developer District495 in collaboration with revered architect Christian Zapatka, delivering a masterpiece where quality and attention to detail take center stage. Zapatka has completely re-imagined the architecture of this storied property, combining timeless Georgetown elegance with District495’s impeccable craftsmanship to create a highly functional 21st century dwelling that pays homage to its past while fit for modern luxury living.

The home’s ageless design incorporates the principles of Beaux Arts planning, establishing a floor plan that is both logical and graceful. At the heart of the home is a central curvilinear staircase, capped by a skylight, and an elevator that connects all four levels. The details throughout this custom-built 3,100 square foot, 4-bedroom, 4 full bathroom, 2 half bathroom home recall the sophistication of townhome craftsmanship in Mayfair, London, and the Upper East Side of New York.

The ground level welcomes with a proper entrance hall paved in custom marble, guiding guests to explore throughout the home’s halls of history. Aptly referred to as “piano nobile,” the main level epitomizes grace and grandeur with soaring 10-foot ceilings and a handsome drawing room anchored by custom millwork, a stately fireplace, and a wall of meticulously restored vintage windows and doors that access a balcony overlooking the property’s breathtaking garden below. The chef’s kitchen is adorned with a large island, beverage bar, Thermador appliances, an ILVE Majestic range, and fixtures from Waterworks and Visual Comfort. The top level is dedicated to two private suites, including an indulgent owner’s oasis. Multifunctionality extends throughout the lower level with a full guest suite, home office, and wine storage.

Dual stairways lend a commanding entry to a vast and manicured garden, a coveted treasure amongst the home’s urban setting. A garage, complete with an EV charger, doubles as a garden pavilion with beautiful glass French doors, offering a sought-after urban amenity that honors the home’s architectural integrity.

Every detail of this exquisite renovation has been thoughtfully curated and precisely executed to deliver a residence that encapsulates the rich history of its past and the luxurious functionality of the future.
